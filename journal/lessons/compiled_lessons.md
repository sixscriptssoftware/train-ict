## Jan 15 Deep Dive: PSYCHOLOGY BREAKDOWN

### The Numbers That Hurt

| Scenario | Outcome |
|----------|---------|
| GU: 1 entry at 1.34285, 20 pip stop, TP at 1.3385 | **+46 pips = ~$345 profit** |
| GU: What actually happened (8 entries) | **-28 pips = -$234 loss** |
| **Difference** | **$579** |

Same day. Same pair. Same thesis. **$579 difference.**

### Psychology Triggers Identified

1. **FOMO** - "I don't want to miss this move"
   - Reality: You can't catch every move. Better to miss 10 pips of entry than get stopped 3 times.

2. **Ego/Proving Right** - "I KNOW this is going down"
   - Reality: Being right doesn't matter if you can't capitalize. The market rewards discipline, not ego.

3. **Revenge Re-entry** - "I'll just get back in, it's going my way"
   - Reality: Each re-entry without NEW confirmation is gambling, not trading.

4. **Desperation** - Trade #5 was a BUY when thesis was SHORT
   - Reality: Counter-trend trades when you're frustrated = emotional trading.

5. **Tight Stop Syndrome** - 1.5-4.5 pip stops getting eaten
   - Reality: GU has 3-5 pip spread. A 2 pip stop is basically free money for market makers.

### The TWO STRIKE RULE (New)

**If you get stopped twice on the same thesis/pair/session:**
1. STOP TRADING THAT PAIR
2. Wait for next session (London â†’ NY, or NY â†’ next day London)
3. Require NEW confirmation before re-entry
4. No exceptions. This is non-negotiable.
